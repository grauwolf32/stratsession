# Black Hat Europe 2025

**ExCeL London · December 8–11, 2025**

Four-day program; main conference Dec 10–11. **Arsenal on Dec 10–11** with **55 in-person tool demos + 6 labs**, spanning reverse engineering, exploitation, ethical hacking, and cloud security. The Arsenal selection had a noticeable AI-security skew this year.

## Arsenal — AI-security highlights

Press coverage and Medium recap surfaced these AI-security-leaning Arsenal items (verify against Black Hat's official Arsenal page when sourcing):

- **AI-Infra-Guard** (Tencent) — AI infrastructure security analyzer.
- **Harbinger** (Mandiant) — AI red-team / threat-modeling tool from Mandiant.
- **MIPSEval** (Stratosphere IPS) — model-evaluation / IPS pipeline.
- **SQL Data Guard** (Thales Group) — database security tool with AI-driven detection.
- **+ four additional AI-security-focused Arsenal entries** per Medium recap.

These complement the AppSec/offensive Arsenal items, which (per Black Hat's official content-lineup release) cover reverse engineering, exploitation, ethical hacking, and cloud-security domains.

> Many of the same tools shown at BH USA 2025 Arsenal (Aug) re-appear at BH Europe Arsenal (Dec) — confirm via the toolswatch repo `tools/Europe/2025/` directory.

## Strategic read

BH Europe is **smaller and more research-oriented** than BH USA, but it is the year-end consolidation point for European research. Three things to watch for in the Dec 2025 + Jan 2026 publication wave:

1. **AI-Infra-Guard adoption** — Tencent's tool is positioned at the AI-infrastructure layer most other vendors are not addressing yet.
2. **Mandiant Harbinger** — first major-vendor open-source AI red-team release (vs. the Promptfoo / GARAK / PyRIT trio that came from labs).
3. **Cross-pollination with KubeCon NA 2025** — the December timing means BH EU Arsenal effectively gets to integrate the November KubeCon themes (Kyverno's new policy types, Copacetic, MCP-aware service mesh).
