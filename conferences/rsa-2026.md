# RSA Conference 2026

**Moscone Center, San Francisco · March 23–26, 2026**

35th anniversary edition. Scale: ~44,000 attendees, ~700 speakers, ~600 exhibitors, ~400 press. Less open-source-tooling-focused than DEF CON / KubeCon, but the **single largest signal for industry direction** — vendor announcements, alliances, and government posture all converge here.

## Trending topics at RSAC 2026

Per opening and closing press releases:
1. **Agentic AI** — top-of-fold theme. Both offensive (Charlotte AI AgentWorks ecosystem from CrowdStrike, with launch partners Accenture, AWS, Anthropic, Deloitte, Kroll, NVIDIA, OpenAI, Salesforce, Telefónica Tech) and defensive ("autonomous SOC" pitches across vendors).
2. **Offensive vs. defensive cyber capability balance** — re-framing as the previous "AI for defenders" narrative collided with public AIxCC results (Aug 2025) showing AI is competitive for vuln-discovery+patch.
3. **Resilience in a rapidly-evolving threat landscape** — emphasis on architecture-level resilience rather than vendor stacks.

## What to do with RSAC 2026

Treat the conference as a **market-signal scan**, not a tool-shopping list:

1. **Map your AI agent risk to AgentFlayer / agent-takeover research from BH USA 2025 + fwd:cloudsec.** RSAC vendor messaging will lag the actual attack research by ~6 months; act on the research, not the marketing.
2. **The "OSS-from-vendors" trend is accelerating** (Cisco DefenseClaw, prior vendor releases). Evaluate licenses carefully — many are source-available, not OSI-approved.
3. **PQC migration timelines firmed up in 2026.** OpenSSL + NIST FIPS-module updates here are the canonical reference points for production crypto migration planning.
